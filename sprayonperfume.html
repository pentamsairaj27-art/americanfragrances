How to spray perfume the right way

Smell good, feel great - Learn more!



Perfumers create harmonious blends called "accords" by combining different fragrance notes. Common accords include floral, oriental, and woody. These accords define the overall character and style of a fragrance.


1. Clean and Moisturize Your Skin

Before applying perfume, it’s crucial to start with a clean slate. Shower or bathe to cleanse your skin of any previous scents or oils. Once your skin is clean, apply an unscented moisturizer. Moisturized skin helps to lock in the fragrance, making it last longer. Unscented moisturizer ensures that it doesn’t compete with your perfume, allowing the true scent to shine.

2. Apply to Pulse Points

Perfume and cologne react with your body’s natural heat. This is why applying them to pulse points is so effective. Pulse points are areas where blood vessels are closest to the skin, generating more heat and helping the scent to develop and project. The primary pulse points to target include:

  • Wrists: A traditional spot that is easy to apply and frequently exposed.
  • Neck: Especially the sides of your neck, to allow the fragrance to waft up.
  • Behind the ears: This spot helps the scent to be more subtle and intimate.
  • Inside of elbows: Helps to diffuse the fragrance with every arm movement.
  • Back of knees: Ideal for longer-lasting scent diffusion, especially if you’re wearing a dress or shorts.

3. Spray Sparingly

When it comes to applying perfume, less is more. Over-applying can lead to an overwhelming and cloying scent, which can be off-putting. A light touch ensures that the fragrance is pleasant and noticeable without being overpowering. Generally, one or two sprays on the pulse points are sufficient.

4. Consider Your Hair and Clothing

For a fragrance that lingers even longer, you can lightly spray your hair and clothing. Hair holds scent well, releasing it gradually throughout the day. However, be cautious with delicate fabrics, as some fragrances can stain or damage them. To avoid concentrated spots that might cause damage, spray from a distance to ensure even distribution.

5. Layer Your Scents

To enhance the longevity and complexity of your fragrance, consider layering. Use products from the same scent line, such as body wash, lotion, and perfume. Layering helps to build a more robust and lasting fragrance experience.

6. Know When to Reapply

Perfume doesn’t last forever, so you might need to reapply it throughout the day. Carry a travel-sized bottle with you for touch-ups. However, reapply sparingly and focus on the pulse points to avoid overwhelming those around you.

7. Storage Matters

How you store your perfume can affect its longevity. Keep your bottles in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and extreme temperatures. This helps to preserve the integrity of the fragrance.

Spraying perfume the right way can make a significant difference in how long the scent lasts and how it projects. By following these steps, you can ensure that your perfume enhances your presence and leaves a memorable impression. Remember, the key is to apply it strategically and sparingly, allowing your scent to be pleasant and inviting.
